## LinkedIn-style draft

A queue is not an owner.

A lot of care coordination failures happen because the system has tasks, dashboards, and alerts — but no clear owner for the patient journey.

“Someone will get to it” is not ownership.
“Assigned to the team” is not ownership.
“Visible in the queue” is not ownership.

Every patient needs a primary owner and a backup owner. Every facility relationship needs the same. Not because humans should manually chase everything, but because escalation paths have to exist before the first miss.

Ownership is what turns a workflow from passive tracking into active coordination.

The patient should never have to guess who is responsible for the next step. The facility staff should not have to hunt across systems to know who is handling it. And leadership should not need a meeting to discover where the handoff broke.

Dashboards show work.
Owners move work.
