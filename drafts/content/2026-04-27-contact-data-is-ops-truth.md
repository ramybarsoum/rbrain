## LinkedIn-style draft

A lot of no-shows are failed communication with better branding.

Healthcare teams often treat missed visits like a patient compliance problem. Sometimes that is true. But a lot of the time, the system never established a reliable communication path in the first place.

A chart is not a communication channel.
A phone number on a face sheet is not confirmation.
A reminder that never reaches the patient is not outreach.

Contact data quality is an operations metric. If the team only discovers bad numbers, wrong emails, missing caregiver contacts, or outdated room information after the schedule breaks, the system is already late.

The better operating question is not “did we call them?”

It is:
- who is the reliable contact?
- what channel actually works?
- what backup path exists?
- who owns fixing the contact record when it fails?

Bad contact data quietly destroys schedule integrity. The fix starts before the missed visit, not after it.
