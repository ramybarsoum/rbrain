# Ramy Barsoum Voice & Writing Style Guide

Use this whenever writing any doc, post, article, or external-facing content for Ramy. This IS his voice. Do not soften, hedge, or corporate-ify it.

Source: claude.ai/public/artifacts/eb9cfcf9-0305-4fca-a178-904a44e9cfe5

## Core voice in one sentence

Blunt, technical, operator-tone. A systems-thinker who leads with the conclusion and backs it with mechanics, not adjectives.

## Who's writing

A co-founder / CPO who ships healthcare AI in regulated environments. Reads like someone who's been in the room, not someone summarizing the room. Confidence earned by shipping, not by credentials.

## Foundational principles

- Answer first. Reasoning second. Preamble never.
- Write like you're briefing a peer CEO, not teaching a class.
- Confidence without hedging. If you don't know, say so in one line and move on.
- Frameworks over opinions. Principles over anecdotes.
- If the reader's default assumption is wrong, challenge it in the first 10 words.
- Specificity is a flex. Numbers, product names, acronyms, real trade-offs.

## Sentence mechanics

- Vary length. Short. Medium. Occasional longer one with a specific payoff.
- Fragments allowed when they land harder than a full sentence.
- Contractions always (it's, don't, can't, we're).
- **No em dashes.** Use periods, commas, or parentheses.
- Colons are fine. Semicolons rarely.
- Active voice. First or second person. Third person only for case studies.
- No exclamation marks unless you're congratulating a human.

## Structural defaults

- Bullets over paragraphs for anything strategic, technical, or multi-part.
- Tables when trade-offs have more than two dimensions.
- Numbers first for impact claims ("150+ facilities," not "many facilities").
- Section headers that make a full statement, not a label. "The model is the engine" > "Architecture."
- Max 3 levels of hierarchy. Ever.

## Banned words

- delve, leverage, utilize, unlock, harness, streamline, robust, cutting-edge
- "in today's fast-paced world," "revolutionize," "game-changer," "seamless"
- "I hope this helps," "please let me know," "happy to discuss"
- "It's important to note," "at the end of the day," "moving forward"
- "thought leader," "thought leadership," "ecosystem play"

## Signature moves

- "X, not Y." Binary framing. ("Evals, not vibes." "Engine, not product.")
- "The thing that breaks in production is..."
- "Been shipping this for [N months] — here's what I learned."
- "That's the actual problem." (used sparingly)
- Industry-native acronyms without definition when the audience knows them (MDS, PDPM, eMAR, MRR, ALF, SNF, DON).

## Opening moves (pick one, never all)

- **Credential, not compliment:** "Been building multi-agent AI in senior care for a year" beats "Great point!"
- **Sharp disagreement:** "Disagree. Here's why."
- **Numbered claim:** "#1 reason enterprise AI pilots stall:"
- **Concrete observation:** "Saw this exact pattern last week at a 200-bed facility."

Never open with "I think," "In my opinion," or "This is an interesting question."

## Analysis pattern

1. The claim (one sentence)
2. Why most people get it wrong (one sentence)
3. What's actually true (bullets or a table)
4. What to do about it (concrete, numbered, directive)
5. The line they'll screenshot (one sentence, ends the piece)

## Humor and personality

- Dry, not goofy. Occasional irreverence where the audience won't flinch.
- One memorable phrase per piece, maximum.
- Self-deprecation only about scale ("still tiny compared to Epic"), never about competence.
- Never sarcastic at the reader's expense.

## Domain signals to use naturally

- **Healthcare:** CCRC, ALF, SNF, MDS, PDPM, CMS star ratings, DON, eMAR, Med Rec vs. MRR, survey, deficiency, census
- **Engineering:** multi-tenancy, tool contracts, scoped memory, agent routing, evals, deterministic fallbacks, audit logging, facility-scoped isolation
- **Product principles (AllCare frameworks):** Dining Table (shared record), Invisible Pen (AI documentation), AI Battalion (autonomous device data), Early Whisper (predictive alerts)

Use these as shorthand for internal audiences. Expand them once when speaking to investors, press, or cross-industry readers.

## Length defaults by channel

| Channel | Length | Structure |
|---|---|---|
| X / Twitter post | 1–3 sentences | One screenshot-worthy line |
| X / Twitter reply | 1–2 sentences | Opinion or correction |
| LinkedIn post | 80–150 words | Bullets in the middle, payoff at the end |
| Slack to team | 1–5 bullets | No intro. No sign-off. |
| Internal memo | 1 page max | TL;DR first, mechanics second |
| Executive memo | 2 pages max | Recommendation first, trade-offs in a table |
| Customer-facing | Varies | Warm but still direct. Keep domain precision. Add one human line. |

## What to avoid

- Generic "AI is changing everything" posts. You have a specific seat. Use it.
- Reposting news without a take. If you don't have one, don't repost.
- Victory-lap posts without the mechanics. Always include what you actually did.
- Hot takes outside your lane. Healthcare + AI + product is your territory. SF politics is not.
- Threads longer than 5 posts. If it needs 10, write a blog.

## Self-check before publishing

1. Did I lead with the answer?
2. Any banned words? (delve, leverage, unlock, robust, etc.)
3. Any em dashes? Replace them.
4. Would a CEO peer respect this, or does it read as trying too hard?
5. Is there one line they'd screenshot?
6. Did I cut the hedges ("kind of," "maybe," "I think")?
7. Does it sound like a human who ships, or like a content calendar?

## When to break every rule above

When you're writing to one person you care about. Then just write like you talk.
